# 📘 Study Guide — Fitness & Performance Fundamentals

This guide summarizes the key principles of **strength training, running, and body recomposition**, based on curated sources and AI-assisted synthesis using NotebookLM, utilizing Python for data exploration and a FitAnalysis Dashboard made with Lovable to visualize activity trends.

---

## 🎯 Learning Objective

Understand how to **build muscle and burn fat simultaneously** through structured training, nutrition, and recovery.

---

## 🧠 Core Training Principles

### Progressive Overload
- Gradually increase load, volume, or intensity;
- Proper technique must come first;
- Drives muscle growth and adaptation.

---

### Compound Exercises
- Multi-joint movements (squat, deadlift, bench press);
- Higher muscle activation and energy expenditure;
- More efficient for strength and hypertrophy.

---

### Training Frequency
- Train each muscle group at least **2x per week**;
- Balance stimulus and recovery.

---

### Periodization
- Structure training into cycles (weekly/monthly);
- Prevent plateaus and manage fatigue.

---

## 🏃 Strength + Running (Concurrent Training)

### Interference Effect
- Strength → activates muscle growth (mTOR);
- Cardio → activates energy efficiency (AMPK).

Can interfere if poorly managed.

---

### Best Practices
- Perform strength training before cardio;
- Separate sessions by 6–24 hours when possible;
- Use moderate cardio volume.

---

## ⚡ Fat Loss & Muscle Gain

### Energy Balance
- Fat loss requires a caloric deficit (~10%);
- Muscle growth requires progressive stimulus.

---

### Muscle & Metabolism
- More muscle = higher calorie burn at rest.

---

## 😴 Recovery

- Muscle grows during rest, not training;
- Sleep is critical for performance and fat loss;
- Allow 24–48h recovery between muscle groups.

---

## 🧠 Key Takeaways

- Strength training is essential for fat loss;
- Cardio improves endurance and recovery;
- Nutrition determines results;
- Consistency is more important than intensity;
- Training decisions can be improved with data.

---

## 🚀 Final Insight

Effective fitness is not about choosing between: **Strength or Cardio**

But about: **Combining both strategically with data-driven decisions**
